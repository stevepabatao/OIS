Online Marketing


There are two main categories of online marketing. There is paid search, or PPC, and there is organic search, or SEO. All online marketing methods will fall under one of these two umbrellas in one form or another; whether it's social media, blogging, or anything else.


What is PPC?


Watch Video: What is PPC and SEO?


PPC stands for Pay-Per Click. PPC is the paid form of online marketing. The pros of doing PPC are immediate traffic from credible sources and instant data. The biggest con, besides having to pay for your traffic, is that you only show up on the search engine you signed up for. With PPC if you want to show up on other search engines you'll have to setup a paid advertising campaign with them. Scaling your traffic or showing up on additional search engines using PPC can be somewhat complicated and very expensive if not monitored and optimized correctly.


What is SEO?


SEO stands for Search Engine Optimization. SEO is basically improving your popularity with the search engines to show up for keywords you want. The biggest pro of SEO, besides free 24/7 traffic, is that your rankings will be similar across all of the major search engines since they all use similar methods for tracking and ranking sites. The other nice thing about SEO is that once you start to rank well for one keyword you'll gradually start to rank for other related keywords with minimal, to no change in your SEO strategy. The con of SEO is that it does take time and the slow process can, at times, be frustrating. Just be patient and continue to work daily.  SEO is not immediate. SEO will take some time but once you crack into the top 10 search results and slowly move up to the #1 position, you'll enjoy a consistent flow of FREE, long term traffic and sales.


How Does SEO Work?


To understand how SEO works let's first get a better understanding of what a search engine is. A search engine is a complicated algorithm whose goal is to match user's searches with the most relevant sites. Essentially, a computer match maker. In order to determine the relevancy of a site the search engine will look at three main areas, site architecture, content, and links. After we understand what's important to a search engine we can use that to our advantage and get our site ranking more quickly. So let's take a closer look at each one of those areas starting with site architecture.


What is Site Architecture?


Watch Video: Why Site Architecture is Important


Site architecture is mostly about how your site functions, how it links within itself, and how things are labeled, for example your meta tags. A good site architecture will have an easy to use navigation with links throughout the page that connect it with the rest of the site that makes it easy for the user to quickly find what they're looking for. It will also have unique meta tags, titles, descriptions, and keywords on the important pages they're trying to rank for.


Having unique meta tags helps the search engine know which page is the most relevant to a user after a search has been made. For example, think about how a book is written with it's different chapters and unique headings. This is done so the reader can get an idea of what the chapter is about and to keep the book organized. If all of the chapter headings were the same, "Chapter 1- The Beginning", it would make things extremely difficult to navigate and confusing to read. This same concept applies to your website. Your site is the book, the pages represent the chapter headings, and the reader represents the search engine. Unique meta tags will help the search engines better know what to rank you for and get an idea of what is important about each page so it can send the right traffic to the it.


What kind of Content do I Need?


Watch Video: How Unique Content Helps Rank Your Site


Content should be both user and search engine friendly. User friendly implies that it's grammatically correct, reads well, and pertains to the industry your marketing to. Search engine friendly means that it relates to your niche and industry and contains keywords you want to rank for.


Search engines like seeing unique information and lots of it. Having unique content on each page that represents your niche or industry is a must in terms of ranking for specific keywords. Search engines also like seeing new content uploaded to a site on a consistent basis. Whether it's once a week or once a month, consistency is the key. Remember, the more often you post the faster you'll rank for your keyword terms. For example, let's compare it to a fine dining restaurant. Fine dining restaurants have beautiful menus that are divided into specific sections. In each section you'll find items that all relate, have a title, and a description of what it comes with. One common thing you'll find among fine dining restaurants is that they also have a daily, weekly, or seasonal special entree which helps keep patrons coming back. This same concept applies to your site. The restaurant is your site, the sections are your pages, the new entrees being fresh content, and the patrons being the search engines and users. New content on a regular basis is one of the keys to keeping both search engines and users coming back again and again.


What are Backlinks?


Watch Video: What are Links and How to Get Them


Backlinks are links on other websites that point back to your site.  A backlink (sometimes just referred to as a "link") is also a way for a search engine to determine how popular you are in your given niche and industry. The more links you have the more popular your site becomes and the higher it climbs in the search engine rankings. You can think of a link as a vote of confidence towards your site. This also means there are different varying degrees of links you can get.


What are the Best Links?


The best links come from sites that relate to your niche or industry and that are already popular with the search engines. For example, let's compare it to high school popularity. Joe is a 17 year old junior. He's mediocre in all of his subjects and likes to play football. Tom is an 18 year old senior. Everyone knows and respects Tom as the captain of the football team and 4.0 honors student. After Tom meets Joe, Tom tells everyone how cool Joe is. Tom's influence has now rubbed off on Joe and everyone that likes Tom now likes Joe.


In this example Joe represents your site, Tom is a related popular site, and the rest of the students are the search engines. This one vote of confidence from Tom could potentially outweigh 10, 50, or even a 100 other votes from semi-popular students. A link from a popular site tells the search engines that your site is relevant and should be popular as well.  You want as many of those valuable links as you can get.  It's also important to have links from a variety of sources. In the example above, this would mean having Joe make new friends with some students from different clicks; jocks, cheerleaders, skaters, people in band, etc. These won't necessarily outweigh the popularity of the one vote from Tom, but it does make Joe appear friendlier, more natural and still increases his popularity. This type of popularity is what the search engines want and expect to see in a high ranking website.

What is Commenting?

Commenting is one of the easiest ways to generate relevant links and referring traffic to your site. Commenting is simply the process of finding websites that allow you to include a comment on their page and then leaving a relevant comment AND a link to your own website. By leaving a useful comment on blogs you'll not only get a link back to your own website but you'll also avoid your comment being deleted and develop a good reputation with the site owner; which will be very useful for when we introduce a marketing strategy called Guest Posts in Month 3.

How do I Comment?

Commenting takes very little time once you have a handle on the do's and don'ts. The hardest part about commenting is simply finding relevant places to post a comment. We'll address each of these in the order they were given.

First, the dos
  • Read before you comment. Read the article or post and skim through some of the comments to get an idea of what to say. Blanket statements of gratitude or anything that doesn't relate to the article is a sure fire way to get your comment deleted.

  • Provide insight and value. Try to include something about the post like the article's title in your comment. Your comment should also be something that contributes to the article or discussion.

  • Link to your site. The whole point of a comment is to get a link back to your site.  When it makes sense, try to include your link using the keyword you want to rank for, aka an anchor text link.

Commenting is like having a good conversation. We actively listen to get a better understanding of the situation before giving out our two cents on the matter. When you first land on a new site take a few minutes to read and/or skim through the article and some of the comments. This will give you an idea of what behavior is acceptable by the author so that when you leave a comment it won't be deleted.

One thing to note for commenting is the no-follow and do-follow sites.

No-follow sites simply mean they don't directly pass link authority to your site, whereas do-follow allows it. While do-follow links are the preferred sites to get links from, this does not mean no-follow links are not valuable. On the contrary, no-follow links, depending on the topic, may send you more referring traffic which could outweigh the value in terms of sales and traffic, than you may get from a single link. Also, even though the link isn't directly recognized by the search engines it still counts as a link.

Articles can be used as another form of Linking. A well written article will help promote your site and give you a link back to it, aka a backlink.

How do I Write a Good Article?

First and foremost, the content you write should relate to the page you're trying to promote. It should also contain the keywords you're trying to rank for within reason. Do not saturate your article with your targeted keyword.

For example, in a 800 word article you could include your keyword twice, somewhere in the beginning and another in the middle. You should also include the keyword in your title when it makes sense, like commenting, this can usually be done if you take a minute to think about it. If not, don't stress over it just move on to the next article.

Your article, like your commenting, should be informative and useful. To help you create an informative article think of some good questions you could use and then answer them. Your article does not have to be complicated. Often the simple things are the ones most valuable, so don't take for granted what you assume everyone knows.

When writing, write out your complete thoughts before stopping to go back and edit them. Its often easier to keep writing when you're not worried about the structure, plus that's what the editing stage is for.

With your completed article be sure to include a link back to your site in the Resource or Bio box. In most cases you'll be allowed to leave two links. When you have the option to leave two links put the first to your home page and the second to the page you're trying to rank. If you're only allowed one, just alternate between your home page and the page you're trying to rank. When possible use an anchor text link with your keyword. Once that's done it's time to post it to an article directory to start getting some links.

What is PPC?

Pay per click (PPC) is an Internet advertising model used on websites, where advertisers pay
their host only when their ad is clicked. With search engines, advertisers typically bid on keyword phrases relevant to their target market. Content sites commonly charge a fixed price per click rather than use a bidding system.


What are the Benefits?


Watch Video: Get Instant Traffic with PPC


Instant traffic and conversions. Normally, getting a new site traffic early on is impossible because it takes time to start to get your site ranked because of all the competition. PPC levels the playing field by allowing everyone to show up for the keywords they want at a minor advertising cost.

Besides instant traffic and creating conversions, you're also developing data. Data is the key to making your site better and increasing your conversions. Understanding where your traffic is coming from and what people are doing on your site will help you zero in on the strengths and weaknesses of your site.


How do I Get Started?

The first thing you'll need to do is create an account with the search engine you would like to advertise on- we recommend Google Adwords to begin, but there are others. After you've created an account it's time for some keyword research.


Keyword Research

Keyword Research is extremely important because your campaign only gets one first impression with the search engines, and while you can make changes later on, the first impression has a huge impact on how your site will start off. So take your time in this phase to make sure you've gotten all of the keywords you want.

One of the best tools you can use to help you find keywords that relate to your niche is the Google Keyword Tool. This tool will help you come up with new ideas for keywords and give you an idea of the competition and how much you would have to spend to be competitive.

When using the Google Keyword tool it's often best to type in a simple search, select the boxes that best apply to your niche, and then export the results. Do this a few times to get a chunk of keywords you can use. This tool will also help you find good negative keywords to add to your list. Once you have a good size list its time to work organizing them into groups.


What kind of Keywords Should I Get?

When you're first developing your keyword list you'll want to start with a combination of long tail and short tail keywords focused around your niche and related keywords. As time goes by you'll slowly be removing and adding new keywords to this list based on the data you're getting back.

You should also add a few Negative Keywords to your campaign. Adding negative keywords will help refine the traffic you're getting and potentially increase your conversions. Some standard negative keywords to add, depending on your niche of course, would be, "free", "scam", and "review". The first two might be self explanatory, but the third one is generally added because it's a keyword that indicates the user is still researching the product or service they're looking to buy.


How Should I Organize My Keywords?

Watch Video: Creating the Best First Impression with the Right Keywords


All of your keywords will need to be grouped in ad groups. You'll be grouping like terms and those that share many of the same keywords together. Doing this will allow you to create specific ads targeting what your users are searching for. To illustrate this point, think of a fruit field that has red and green apples, pears, peaches, and cherries. The field itself represents all of the different keywords that you've chosen that best represent your site and keyword niche (long tail and short tail keywords). Although all of the fruits share the same field they should be separated into their likenesses so that people can find what they're looking for easier. The red apples with the red apples, the peaches with the peaches, and so forth. The great thing about being organized is that you're now going to easily see which areas of your PPC campaign is doing the best, and which parts need some pruning.


When it comes to the pruning side of things, this is a great chance to add additional negative keywords to your campaign. As you review your data you'll start to find keywords that don't relate to your niche. Adding negative keywords to your account will help refine the traffic you're getting while increasing the visitors.


In the beginning of the campaign you'll want to keep the keywords on broad and phrase. As time goes by you'll be able to determine which of the Match Types for that keyword performs the best and then you can pause the other. Using broad and phrase match types in the beginning will allow you to find additional keywords users are typing in to find your site. A Broad Match keyword is one that will show your ad if the keywords are all used in a search. For example, if our keyword is, organic green apples, our ad will show if a user types in, organic cherries peaches and green apples for sale, because it used all of the keywords we had. A Phrase Match keyword is one that will show our ad only if the phrase is contained in the search. For example, if someone types in buy organic green apples and pears, our ad will show; however, if they were to type in, buy organic green and red apples, our ad would not show. Exact Match is what it means, the search must match the exact search made in order to show the ad.


Ad Creation

The most important thing next to choosing the right keywords and getting them into the right ad groups, is creating a good ad. Without a good ad you're looking at minimal quality traffic, whereas the right ad can send you high quality traffic.


Ad Creation Tips:


Creating a good ad qualifies the traffic and gives them an incentive to come to your site. A good ad also clearly represents the offer and gives the user an idea of what they'll find on the page after they click the ad, remember "clarity trumps persuasion". For example, if we're trying to sell red apples we may do some research by seeing what others are advertising using similar keywords that we've chosen in our red apples ad group. The ads that show up will help give you ideas on things you may consider adding to your own ads. It will also help you see ways in which
you could standout and be unique.

Your ad starts with a Headline. The headline needs to be catchy and in general it should also include the keyword term that best fits the ad group it's being written for. And don't forget that whatever you use should accurately represent your site. For example, if we're writing ads for our red apples ad group we know that all of those keywords will use "red apples" in them so we'll include red apples in the title, "Delicious Organic Red Apples", "Always Fresh Red Apples", "Why Red Apples are Best", "Best in State Red Apples", etc.

Now that you've hooked a reader its time to make your copy shine by taking the headline a step further and grabbing the attention of the user and enticing them to click through to your site. Every ad you create should have some type of Call to Action, for example, "Buy Here", "One Night Only", etc.

Once the copy is complete take a look at your Display URL. In some cases you can touch it up a little in order to show off your keyword again, red apples. The reason we want the keyword term "red apples" in the header and copy, and display URL if you can, is because when a search is made the keywords that match the users search will be bolded and the more your ad is bolded the better chance it has of being seen and clicked on.

After you've gotten the display URL all setup you'll want to find the best page to send the traffic to. Use the Destination URL to point the users that click on your ad to the most relevant page of your site. Using the example we introduced about fruit, if people are just looking for good fruit, send them to the home page where they can get an overview of what you have to offer. If they typed in something that pulled up your peach keywords then that ad should send them to your peach section, and so on.

Once your ad is complete it's time to copy and change something about it. It is vital to the success of your marketing campaign, both SEO and PPC, to be continually testing different variables to determine what works best. A simple A B test can be changing the header you created on the copy to a dynamic one. A Dynamic Header will change to the keyword search made if the character limits allow it. For example, if our header is "Why Red Apples are Best" and we put it into a dynamic header, {Keyword:Why Red Apples are Best}, when a user searches for "Buy Delicious Red Apples", because it fits under the 25 character limit our header will display "Buy Delicious Red Apples"; however, if the user typed in Buy Delicious Red Apples in Bulk, our ad would display what's in between the brackets, "Why Red Apples are Best}.

Another thing you may want to consider as you do A B Testing is the appeal to human nature. What does that mean? Once you have your ad written you can re-write to appeal to envy, fear, greed, or exclusiveness, for example, "Make the Best Red Apple Pie Tonight and Be the Talk of the Neighborhood". This ad focuses on the envy side, whereas, "Award Winning, Best in State Red Apples! Get 'em Before They're Gone", focuses on the fear of missing out.

Before you submit your ad keep in mind the Capitalization. Marketing is all about being see in the right place. One way to help your ad stand out and be seen among the competition is by capitalizing the beginning of each word as you may have noticed in the examples we've shown you above. The other way is to use question marks and the Exclamation point to grab the attention of the searcher to your ad. You can only use one exclamation point per ad, so make sure you choose the spot that makes it look the best.

Never delete a keyword because when it's removed so is the data. Later on in the coming months when you're looking to add more keywords to your account you may not remember which ones you removed. Leaving the keyword paused will keep its history and let you see that you already used the keyword once before and that it didn't work out for the account.


How do I know when to change something?  First the obvious; if it's been a month, or you have significant data to show that a keyword is getting clicks but isn't creating any conversions you have two options, pause the keyword, or create a new ad group where you can make an ad specifically targeting the keyword and see if it does any better. If you notice that you're getting a lot of sales from a particular group of keywords, see if you can add more keywords to it, and/or add some of the same keywords with different match types for testing.


Daily Checks

There are a couple areas that you'll want to check on a daily basis, Ads and Keywords. These metrics will help you see how your account is doing.



Ads- Every ad group you've created should have at least two ads running. The ad check is simply seeing which ad is performing the best and to make sure that the ads are actually up and running. There are times when for some reason an ad maybe disapproved or perhaps just stuck on pause. Its important to make sure that your ads are running. Once they're running you'll want to check on the CTR and see if one is doing better then the other. In order to get an accurate test of this, when you setup the ad you'll want to select the option that will display the ads "Evenly". This means, that the search engines will show you're A ad and the the B ad. The standard rotation for ads across the search engines is by performance. Performance is good if you're managing a large amount of ad groups and don't have the time to manually review each group every day.


What you're looking for in the ads is a reason why one is better than the other; this is usually done by comparing the CTRs of both ads. The higher CTR belongs to the best performing ad. If both ads have about the same CTR and it has been long enough to get a lot of data, pick one to pause and create a new ad. If there is a clear difference, no matter how small it is, between one and the other, pause the worst one and copy the best one. You're now going to change one thing about this copy you just made. It could be anything from adding a dynamic header (if you haven't
used that one in a test yet) to changing the call to action.



Keywords- You'll be able to sort your account to see all of the keywords despite what ad group or campaign they're in. The information that you'll want to consider before pausing a keyword is how many impressions it had, did it receive any clicks, and what was its average position. Some keywords could be really low, or have zero clicks, only for the reason that they weren't listed high enough in the search engines because of a poor ad position. This is why when you setup an account for the first time that you slightly over estimate the keyword bids so you can get an accurate test that will allow you to sift through your keyword list and leave only the best ones running. This is also why you start your campaign with only broad and (very few) phrase match keywords, so you can find all of the other related keywords people are typing in to find your site looking for your products or services.


Making Changes

Two weeks have gone by since you first started your account and you've got some good data on your account. You've been watching the traffic go up and down while you've been doing your daily checks on your ads and keywords and now it's time to make some changes. One of the best things you can do is record the changes you make on your account somewhere that you can refer back to. A pad of paper or creating a new document in Word or another writing
program will work. Just be sure you include the date of when you made the changes. Recording what changes you make will be important in helping you see what impact they had on your account, because in two weeks you'll be back again to make more changes at which time you'll adjust the date range to show only the last two weeks. You'll then compare the last two weeks with the two before it and see if the changes you made had a good impact on your account.

If you notice a decrease in traffic, it's most likely because you've refined your traffic by pausing poor performing keywords. What you may have forgotten to do was add in additional keywords to test. Either way, there is no need to be alarmed. Now is the chance you have to make changes to fix it, just remember to write down your changes so you can check and see how they did.

What are Backlinks?

Backlinks are incoming links to a website or page. Backlinks are a huge part of what search engines look at to determine their relevance to a given search. Search engines will also take a look at the anchor text link, the words used create the link, to determine how well it relates to the site its pointing to. This is why it's important that when you create an anchor text link, or get one from another source, that you try to have it use the keyword you want to rank for.


How does Backlinking benefit my site?

Backlinks are seen as a vote of confidence that tells the search engines that you're a trusted authority site on, whatever your niche is. Also, if the site using the anchor text relates to, or is in the same industry, this backlink is seen as an even stronger vote.


How do you get Backlinks?

Getting Backlinks is something that you've been doing this whole time. The methods you've been taught and have been working on were designed to help you generate good quality links that relate to your niche and point back to your site.

The difference that we're going to go over in this section is how you can check on your competitors Backlinks and see how to outrank them.

To get started all you'll need is the URL of a competitor you want to look at. If you don't have one in mind simply type in the keyword, or a keyword, you're trying to rank for and click on one of the top 5 sites that show up and copy their URL.


Tools you'll use:


Watch Video: How to Out Rank Your Competition Using Their Backlinks


There are three tools that you can use to help check on your competitors backlinks, Yahoo Site Explorer, Backlinkwatch.com, and Market Samurai. Two of these are free tools and the third is a paid tool- with a free trial. Let's talk about each of them:


Yahoo Site Explorer- will show you most of the backlinks your competitor has. When using this tool be sure to select, from the Show Inlinks, drop down menu "Except from this domain". This will remove the links that are on the home page of that domain. This removes the links on the domain going to the other pages of the site which don't count as Backlinks.


Backlink Watch- BacklinkWatch is a very old and reliable tool. It will display up to 500 links at a time and also try and show you the anchor text, outbound links (how many links that site is giving out), and in some cases it also lets you know if it's a nofollow link.


Market Samurai-  will tell you everything the first two tools do and the PR of each site. This tool will save you lots of time in figuring out which sites you should look at getting a link from first, and to quickly figure out what you'll need in order to overcome your competition. The only downside is that this is a paid tool.


We recommend you try out both Yahoo Site Explorer and BacklinkWatch so you become familiar with both. If you decide to use Market Samurai, you won't be disappointed but before you do we highly recommend you first become familiar with the other tools. Market Samurai will simply speed up this process and make it much much easier.


Checking Your Competitors Backlinks

Now that you have your competitor's URL go ahead and analyze it in one of the tools. Now all you need to do to create a link is follow what they did. In most cases the links you're competitors used will relate to a comment which is something you have been doing for a long time now and will be able to do with ease.

Knowing where your competitor is getting their links and what PR they are will make it easy for you to overcome your competition because it's as simple as having more links and better content. Using these tools and creating links in the same places as your competitors, while getting links from other sources, will eventually get you out ranking your competitors.

For instance, if you know that your competitor has 100 backlinks and that 10 are PR 5, 50 are PR 3 and the rest are PR 1, then all you need to do to out rank them is get a combination of links that relate to your niche and have a slightly better PR. So, you could get 5 PR 6, 10 PR 5, 50 PR 3, and the rest PR 1 and be seen by the search engines as the more relevant site in your niche, this is of course, if the content on your site is also equally as good as your competitors.

One of the best ways to help promote your site and get a high quality link and traffic back to your site is by writing and submitting a Guest Post to a popular blog that relates to your niche.


What is a Guest Post?

A Guest Post is simply an article that you write which you then submit to the author or web administrator of a blog and ask them to post it on their site. The purpose is to get a high quality link back by selecting those blogs which relate the most to your niche and have a good PR and a number of subscribers who visit the site regularly. Essentially it's the same as Article marketing but instead of posting your article to an article directory, you're posting on blog. And because you're posting to a blog that's typically run by a single person- you need their approval and permission first. By targeted relevant blogs, you'll also get your content in front of that blog's relevant visitors which means more traffic to your own site.

We briefly mentioned Guest Posting when you first learned about Commenting as a strategy. If you recall, the idea with commenting is to build a relationship with the owner of relevant websites/blogs by leaving relevant comments that add to the conversation. After a couple months of commenting- if you were able to find updated blogs related to your niche- then you have the opportunity to contact that site owner and ask if they'd be willing to accept a guest post.


How do I get my Post Accepted?


Watch Video: Guest Posting; Getting Accepted and How to Spin Rejection into a Win Win


One way to find out how to get your post accepted is by doing a bit of Research into the blog you're thinking of submitting your article to. In some cases you'll be able to search and find other guest posts. Skim through a couple of the guest posts, like you did when writing comments, to get a better idea of what kind of things are acceptable to leave in your article. For example, if you're wondering if you can include an anchor text link back to your site and you find that the majority of the other guest posts have them, then you know that's okay to do with this particular blog.


The last two things to keep in mind when writing your guest post are Relevance and Reputation. You want to make sure the article you submit is something that the readers of the blog you're submitting to will find valuable. You also want to make sure that you're not spamming your owns site's reputation in self-promotion, doing so will inevitably get you an email reply saying, no thank you or, I can't use this. The only thing you'll want to leave in your article is a link back to your site. Knowing where to put this link will depend on the research you previously did. In some cases you'll be able to leave an anchor text link, others, you may just have to settle for one link in your "authors" box.



What happens if my Post is Rejected?  

If for some reason your article is rejected, you have a couple options. "Try try again" or re-write and submit. The reality is that there is going to be at least 100 different blogs out there that relate to your site and of those 100 there is most likely 10 that are popular enough for you to submit your article to. If one blog rejected your article, no worries, ask yourself what you could do to make the post better, look at the next blog you want to submit it to, skim a couple of their guest posts, touch up your article as needed, and submit it to them. If it's rejected again, no worries because you can always "re-write and submit" it to your own site and an article directory like EzineArticles or Go Articles after you re-write it. So, it's always a win win situation for you.

Getting Guest Posts will really help improve the traffic and authority of your own site. We encourage you to work on getting one every month. They're a lot easier to get than you might expect, especially if you follow the three rules above: Research, Relevance, and Reputation.
