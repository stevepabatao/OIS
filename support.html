When I click on the videos all I see is a blank page.
If you are using Internet Explorer we need to add the eLibrary to your Trusted Sites. Please follow these steps to do this:
  1. Click on Tools
  2. Click on Internet Options
  3. Click on the Security Tab
  4. Under "Select a zone to view or change security settings" click on Trusted Sites
  5. Click on the Sites button
  6. Under "Add this website to the zone" make sure this sites url is listed
  7. Click on the Add button
  8. Make sure the checkbox next to "Require server verification" is unchecked
  9. Click on the Close button
  10. Click OK
When I click on a video I hear a loud "Click" sound
When clicking on a video please hold down the CTRL button on your keyboard while clicking the link. This action disables any pop-up blocker you may have installed on your computer.
How do I pause the video?
To pause the video, click on the pause button located at the bottom left corner of the video. It is located to the left of the "slider" bar that moves when the video is playing.
How do I download and install the Adobe Flash Player?
  1. Visit Adobe Flash Player to download.
  2. Choose the appropriate Operating System (Windows XP, Mac OSX, etc) and browser (Internet Explorer, Firefox, etc) and click on the appropriate download link.
  3. Once the file downloads to your computer, find where you saved it and double-click it to launch the installer.
  4. Follow the on-screen instructions.
How do I download and install the Mozilla Firefox browser?
  1. Visit Mozilla Firefox to download.
  2. Click the large green download button to get the latest version.
  3. Once the file is finished downloading, find where you saved it on your computer and double-click it to launch the installer.
  4. Follow the on-screen instructions.
When I play the videos they are "choppy" and all i can hear is the sound.
You may experience choppy videos if you have a dial-up connection.
What is the best connection speed I need to be able to view the videos?
Based on the number and size of the videos, we would recommend DSL or Cable.
